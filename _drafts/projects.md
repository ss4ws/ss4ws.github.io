# Desk Everyday Reports 
It is like taking a picture of the same place at the same time everyday but in writing. By doing so you will observe all the things that have been moved around all the things that are in use,all the things the are on top of the one another.

Here is an exemple:

*March 19, 2017* - Note on what’s on my table right now.
On the top left corner: My iPhone on top of a Pierre Bergounioux’s book *Le style comme expérience*, on top of my ipad on top of my pocket wallet. A black pen is hanging out there too. Next to them there is my scarf, my glasses, and in the same vicinity my computer protective case.

On my right the first object is a red mechanical pencil. I brought this pencil recently from Greece and I have since I was eighteen years old. Next to the pen there is Alain Robbe-Grillet’s *Préface à une vie d’écrivain* which sits on top of another book *Rhétorique du texte numérique: Figures de la lecture, anticipations de pratiques* by Alexandra Saemmer. My ear phones are tangled over there too the charger cables and my wool hat. Car keys,house keys, next to each other, Tiger balm, Cheerful balm and Head to toes balm are all spread out at the far right corner of the table.   
 
# Memoire (de Debord) Generator 
Build a website for Guy Debord’s and Asger Jorn’s _Memoires_. _Mémoires_ is a collections of quotes taken from various places and placed arbitrarily on the page splashed with colors. The method used to write it is called _détournement_ invented by the International Situationists. The website will offer one version identical to the printed book. But since the texte is not an original written text by the Author whose name is on the cover, why not imagine a new series of _détournements_ based on the idea of memories? Based on the original structure of the book, each user will be able to use their Twitter account to replace the “original” text of the book to generate their own mémoires. Tweets will not be placed in chronological order but rather randomly on the the text placement of the book creating a chaotique    
We can imagine multiple way to generate text from Twitter: 
1. using one twitter account 
2. using multiple users’ twitter accounts 
3. using a hashtag  
  
# Day 
Although we don't all share the same schedule, our days are divided by the same more or less time slots: mornings, afternoon, evenings, nights. This project divides a day's 24 hours in 4 equal parts of 6 hours each and by using Instagram's and twitter's API posts automatically pictures hash-tagged with words associated with those 4 time slots. The result is a dynamic ever-changing _tableau_ reposting what people post on social media about their day.

Morning: Sunrise, morning, breakfast, coffee, tea, work, etc. 
Noon: Lunch, restaurant, meal, etc. 
Afternoon: traffic, home,  
Evening: Sunset, dinner,

1. Marché 
- est-ce que tous les ingrédients sont faciles à trouver? 
- quels marchants les vendent? 
- quel trajets est-ce qu’on fait pour les trouver? 
- quels échanges on a avec les marchands? 
- ou alors est-ce qu’on trouve tous les ingrédients au super-marché?

2. Partage du repas
-  et avec qui on a partagé le repas (est-ce qu’on a mangé seul ou avec de la compagnie? qui était là? est-ce que la compagnie a aidé à la préparation du repas? de quel façon? quelles était les discussions échangés pendant la préparation ou à la table?  


3. Digestion 
- enregistrer minutieusement le processus de digestion, difficultés, gaz, évacuations etc. 
- comment était la digestion des convives?
